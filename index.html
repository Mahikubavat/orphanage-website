They provide help in areas like education, health and skill development. There are several campaigns being run to spread awareness about orphanage issues. Many states have formulated new policies for the operation of orphanages. These policies aim to protect the rights of children and ensure their well-being.

Orphanages are important because they provide care and support for children who can't be cared for by their biological families. They also help children develop their skills and talents.

How orphanages help children

Provide basic needs: Orphanages provide food, clothing, shelter, and healthcare. Offer education: Orphanages offer formal and non-formal education. Help children develop skills: Orphanages help children develop their skills and talents. Provide a safe environment: Orphanages provide a safe and nurturing environment for children.

Challenges orphanages face

However, many orphanages face challenges, such as not having adequate resources. Growing evidence suggests that long-term stays in institutional care can have detrimental effects on a child's development.

How to help orphaned children

You can help orphaned children by: Supporting organizations that help orphaned children, Sharing happiness in orphanages, Helping children develop their skills and talents, and Helping children break the cycle of despair.
